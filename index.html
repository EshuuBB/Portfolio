Brew Theory — Premium Coffee

Full brand launch for a Nepali high-altitude producer targeting Tokyo, Copenhagen & London specialty cafés
What I made
Positioning · Brand identity · Packaging system · Instagram campaign · Meta ads
Outcome
3× wholesale price lift · 8 cafés committed pre-launch · Positioned for urban specialty market

Problem

Nepal produces exceptional high-altitude coffee — yet almost no one outside the country knows it exists. A family-owned producer in the Kavre highlands had a premium product and zero brand infrastructure. In a $30B+ global specialty coffee market where Ethiopia, Colombia, and Guatemala own the "origin story" category, Nepali coffee was invisible.

Strategy

Framework: Byron Sharp's Distinctiveness Principles. The brand needed distinctive assets that could compete without relying on category cues. White space: no origin coffee brand owned the Himalayan altitude story with scientific credibility. Positioning: "Grown Above The Clouds. Tasted Around The World." Every visual decision encoded altitude as the irreducible distinction.

Visual

Deep espresso brown primary, warm cream negative space, muted terracotta signature accent. Sharp editorial serif wordmark. Geometric mountain mark — flat vector, no gradients, works at 8mm on a bag or 2m on a billboard.

Campaign

"Grown Above The Clouds. Tasted Around The World." A2 portrait poster on dark espresso with editorial serif typography and abstract topography lines. Instagram-first bean close-ups with altitude coordinates. Bold typographic reveal. 250g kraft bag with matte finish, mountain mark at top, origin details in small caps.

Lumera — Luxury Skincare

Full brand launch for a Himalayan botanical skincare line targeting urban Nepali women and the South Asian diaspora
What I made
Brand strategy · Visual identity · Product launch campaign · Influencer activation · TikTok content architecture
Outcome
12K waitlist pre-launch · 4.8× ROAS on launch Meta campaign · Premium shelf positioning

Problem

The South Asian beauty market is split between mass-market herbal brands that own "natural" but not "luxury," and international prestige brands that own "luxury" but not "local." A Kathmandu entrepreneur wanted to launch clean beauty using Himalayan botanicals — targeting urban Nepali women and the diaspora. The tension: how do you signal luxury credibility without Western visual language? And how do you celebrate local ingredients without "Himalayan exotic" cliché?

Strategy

Frameworks: Benefit Laddering and Category Design. The functional benefit: Himalayan botanicals clinically proven. The emotional benefit: pride in South Asian heritage reframed as luxury. The brand idea: "Heritage is not history. It is science." Lumera creates a new category: heritage science beauty.

Visual

Warm ivory signature background, deep saffron anchor colour, muted sage, single gold accent. Devanagari-inspired abstract letterform. Refined serif display, modern sans body. The brand world: warm, luminous, precise — not earthy or folkloric.

Campaign

"Your Heritage. Your Skin." Warm morning light hero imagery — real South Asian faces. 5-pillar TikTok content: Ingredient Stories, Science Girl, Ritual, Heritage Pride, Transparency. Full-funnel Meta. 15 micro-creators across Nepal, India, and the diaspora.

Growth Campaign · Performance Creative

Pulse AI — Fitness App Growth

Full growth campaign for an AI-powered fitness app targeting 100K users across South Asia
What I made
Growth strategy · Meta funnel architecture · Performance creative system · Retention system
Outcome
3× organic installs in 90 days · 2.2× D30 retention vs. category baseline · $0.38 CPI

Problem

An AI-powered fitness app had a strong product — personalised workout plans, real-time form correction, culturally relevant exercise library — but was stuck at 8,000 users. The product worked. Nobody knew it existed. In a market where fitness apps compete with YouTube tutorials and local trainers on Instagram, Pulse AI needed a growth engine that made the value proposition visceral.

Strategy

Framework: Jobs-to-be-Done. The functional job: get fit without a gym. The emotional job: feel like I have a personal trainer I can't afford. The social job: be proud of a lifestyle that fits me. Three acquisition pillars: problem-aware content, social proof from beta users, AI form-correction as the hero differentiator.

Creative

Deep navy primary, electric cyan accent, warm white for readability. Geometric sans for UI. Template system for 20+ creative variations per week — before/after, feature demo, testimonial, educational, urgency.

Campaign

Three phases: Awareness (Meta video ads showing form correction, TikTok "Can an app fix your squat form?" challenge), Activation (in-app referral with shareable progress cards), Retention (personalised D3/D7/D14/D30 sequences with AI-generated progress summaries). YouTube pre-roll for diaspora audience.
